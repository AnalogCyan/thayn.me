Being neurodivergent in a world built for neurotypicals is exhausting. The social scripts, the expected narration of your own thought process, the assumption that if you know something you must be able to explain how you know it. Lately I have been thinking a lot about how my brain actually works, mostly because people keep asking me to explain it, and I keep failing.

For every domain in my life, my brain has built an algorithm over years of knowledge ingestion, hands-on experience, trial and error, and hyperfocus. And that algorithm? It is an **amorphous blob**. I do not know how it works. I just know that if I follow the blob, the thing gets done correctly. If I try to break out of the blob to explain it to someone, everything crashes.

## Compiled, Not Interpreted

Neurotypicals seem to run on interpreted code. They can narrate their thought process in real time because their process _is_ narration. But my brain compiled that shit years ago. I do not have steps. I have pattern recognition engines built through brute force statistical accumulation over thousands of iterations, and they run beautifully until someone demands I pause execution, decompile the binary, and read them the assembly.

Trying to introspect on the blob while it is running is like trying to understand an [Eridian thrum](https://ia902800.us.archive.org/7/items/project-mary-hail/Project%20Mary%20Hail.pdf). It does not work. The algorithm _is_ the execution. The execution _is_ the knowledge.

These blobs are not just expertise. They are **compensatory infrastructure**. I built them to navigate a world that was not designed for neurodivergence. I automated the hard stuff so I could function. Then people ask me to open the hood and describe the engine, not realizing it is held together with procedural glue and pattern weights that have no linguistic form.

## A Mixture of Experts

As mentioned in the intro, it is not just one blob. There are many of them. Many little algorithms all interwound and all with their own special domains. They are **amorphous**. They overlap. They leak into each other. There are no hard walls between them, just regions of density where one pattern dominates.

So when someone interrupts me while I am executing and asks me how or why, they are asking me to halt a dynamic system in motion, freeze overlapping amorphous processes that were never meant to be paused, and produce a coherent narrative from something that has no central log file.

No wonder it breaks.

Because these blobs overlap and leak into each other, patterns cross-pollinate across domains. A novel problem does not always trigger the algorithm you would expect. We are often looked at as strange for this. We jump to seemingly unrelated topics as we think things through. But that is just our brains pattern-matching in a way neurotypicals do not expect. Get enough experience and knowledge in something with its own structure? Slowly a new algorithm is added to the pile. We do not just see the surface details, but the underlying topology.

## The AI Problem

I need to talk about something that has been frustrating me for years.

My writing gets mistaken for AI-generated content. It got especially bad when ChatGPT first blew up. I am not an AI. I am autistic, and this is simply how I talk when I sit down and think through something.

But the parallels between how neurodivergent minds work and how LLMs work are there, aren't they? Weights over rules. Pattern matching at scale. Compiled knowledge. A mixture of experts architecture. Cross-domain structural bootstrapping. It is all there, and I really do not like that. The structural similarities are frightening. Not because I think I am a machine, but because the comparison reveals how reductive the humanity template has always been. If a statistical model built by a corporation can shadow my cognition this closely, what does that say about neurodivergent individuals like myself; what does it say about AI? I know not what to make of it.

All in all, I do not like the AI comparison. I do not want it to be true. But the topology does not lie.
